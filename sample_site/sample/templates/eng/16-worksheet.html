Modals

Special verbs used with the main verb to make the sentence meaningful.

Modal Verbs
can ought to
could shall
may should
might will
must would

I. Fill in the blanks using the correct Modal Verbs:

  1. May I borrow your notebook.
  2. I could ride a horse when I was 5 years old.
  3. could you hold this for a while?
  4. It may rain today.
  5. would you like some tea.
  6. I will write to you as soon as reach Delhi.
  7. We ought to respect our parents.
  8. You must take care your health.
  9. I can speak German.
  10. Shall I bring a glass of water for you?
  11. We should take care of our pets.
  12. Could you please shut the door?
  13. Shall I make some coffee for you?
  14. It'e getting late. I must go now.
  15. I would like to meet the Principal Ma'am
  16. You can't make fun of others.
  17. You ought to respect your elders.

II. Fill in with Modals - (would/should)

  1. would you pass the bottle of sauce, please.
  2. One should eat only when one is hungry.
  3. You should not get upset so often.
  4. Would you mind giving me your pen for sometime?
  5. The benefits of development should reach the rural people also.
  6. Would you like to come for the trip.
